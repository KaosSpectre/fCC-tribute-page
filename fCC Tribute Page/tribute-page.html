Ada Lovelace

The Mother of Coding

Major Events in Her Life:
  • 10 December 1815 - The birth of Augusta Ada Byron.
  • 1823 - Ada suffered from headaches that obscure her vision.
  • 1828 - Ada, known as the "fairy lady" by Charles Burbage, decides that she wants to fly. She contructs wings from many different materials, and studies the anatomy of birds in order to better determine the ratio of wing to body.
  • 1829 - Ada contracts the Measles. She is bedridden for nearly a year.
  • 1832 - Ada's mathematical abilities begin to emerge. She was privately educated by William Frend, William King, Mary Somerville and Augustus De Morgan. De Morgan taught her much, including the "numbers of Bernoulli" which formed the algorithim for which she became known as The Mother of Coding.
  • 1833 - Ada is introduced to Charles Burbage by Mary Somerville, who was Ada's tutor and friend. He showed her his prototype "Difference Engine" and she was fascinated. Burbage was in turn impressed with Ada's intellect and analytical skills. He dubbed her the "Enchantress of Numbers".
  • 8 July 1835 - Ada married William King.
  • 1836-1839 - Ada has three children, Byron(1836), Anne Issabelle(1837) and Ralph Gordon(1839)
  • 9 Months in 1842-1843 - Ada appended a set of notes with the article written by Luigi Menabrea. In them, she explained the function of the Analytical Engine and her work was very well recieved. The notes were 3 times longer than the article and included the "Note G". In which she showed a method for calculating a set of Bernoulli numbers. This work has caused her to be regarded by many as the first computer programmer.
  • 27 November 1852 - Ada, age of 36, dies of uterine cancer after several months. She is buried next to her father at her request.
